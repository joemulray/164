Most Important Information for CS 164:

  • Boolean Algebra

    • Boolean algebra is the branch of algebra in which the values of the variables are the truth values true and false, usually denoted 1 and 0 respectively. There are four notions, AND, NOT, OR, XOR. Boolean Algebra is the foundation of how computing is operated.

  • The Half Adder

    • The half adder is an example of a simple, functional digital circuit built from two logic gates . The half adder adds to one-bit binary numbers (XY). The output is the sum of the two bits, the sum and the carry. Understanding the Half Adder is very important in understanding how computing works.
